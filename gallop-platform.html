Gallop

Team Project
Figma Drive

Gallop is a web platform that simplifies the horse breeding process by minimizing the effort spent on looking for the perfect horse.

Date
Jan 2023 - April 2023
My role
Design Lead - UI/UX Designer
Team size
4 Developers & 3 Designers

Problems

The horse breeding industry is a significant market that has been growing steadily in recent years. Horse breeders, both professionals and non-professionals, are always looking for ways to improve their breeding programs and produce high-quality horses with desirable traits. However, breeding a horse can be a complicated and time consuming task, involving many factors such as genetics and physical characteristics. Also, without a large network of connections, breeders may struggle to find the best stallion or mare for their breeding programs.

Typography

Asap and Roboto are both modern, easy-to-read sans-serif fonts that complement each other well. For the header Asap has rounded edges and friendly appearance with more organic looks which serves Gallop brand identity to connect with nature. For the body Roboto has clean lines and geometric shapes to look professional and easy to read for the user. The combination of these two fonts is suitable for Gallopâ€™s brand image, which aims to be both welcoming and professional, appealing to both casual horse enthusiasts.

Color Palette

Gallop's color palette combines natural elements with modern design, while also creating a warm, professional and inviting atmosphere. The deep green color provides a strong connection to nature, while the contrast of gray and beige adds a modern and sophisticated touch. Throughout the UI, color contrasts meet's accessibility guidelines to ensure that users with visual impairments can use the app comfortably. The design choices were made to be professional and polished, creating a visually appealing experience that is both functional and user-friendly.

Logo

I have created the logo to be a single line drawing of a horse which is a minimalist and modern representation of the animal to convey simplicity and elegance. This style of artwork also allows for easy scalability and adaptability across different mediums, making it a versatile choice for a brand identity. The use of a single line reflects the idea of a continuous journey, mirroring the relationship between horse and owner.
