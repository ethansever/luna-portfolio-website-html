About Colourfit

Colourfit is a mobile application which helps vision-impaired people identify the colour of their outfits. Through the scanning process, the user will get to know what colour it is and how it compliments their skin/hair tones. Also, Colourfit allows all users who love colour, but have a hard time mix-matching them through user inputs to recommend colour combinations for their current or new styale direction. Gain confidence and a new style perspective by quickly trying different colour combinations today.

Problems

When we take a deep dive into the vision-impaired world, it is clear that people who are vision-impaired, especially when related to colour blindness, can be negatively impacted when building their wardrobe or buying a new piece of clothing. One of the practices they might rely on is by selecting monochromatic or dark tones for their outfit; however, this might result in diminishing their possibilities and ultimately result in losing confidence when choosing different colours for their outfits.

Solution

Based on the research and information gathered, we noticed a market opportunity to develop an application that targets different audiences, but mainly users who struggle with differentiating colours, and also people who have difficulties when trying to match colours. We believe that the act of choosing your outfit must be simple and enjoyable, and through Colourfit.

Competitive Analysis

Colourfit's main competitive advantage focuses on allowing users to scan their own clothes and get recommendations based on their skin tone. Also, allowing the users to organize the items into closets can let the users have easy access to their own items and minimize the amount of time spent on choosing an outfit.

Target Audience

We estimate the target audience will be between 15 and 50 years old, targeting all genders, and the main focus wil be on those users who struggle when choosing an item based on its colour or have any difficulties when differentiating the colours, especially the colour blindness users.

Logo

The black and white “o” represents the experience of individuals with color blindness or other visual impairments. This element symbolizes their perspective and serves as a visual representation of their condition.
By incorporating a colorful “o” alongside the black and white one, the logo acknowledges that Colourfit is an inclusive brand that caters to both visually impaired individuals and those who can perceive colors.
The contrasting colours helps create a visual distinction between elements, making the logo more accessible to everyone. It ensures that the logo remains recognizable and impactful for all viewers, regardless of their visual abilities. This contrast also interprets as a metaphor for the transformative power of Colorfit. Check out all of the UI Kit through here.

Typography

Indivisible and SF Pro Display are two contemporary sans-serif fonts that harmonize seamlessly. Indivisible have sharp edges and a clean, modern aesthetic. SF Pro Display’s rounded edges and polished appearance lead to a professional and sophisticated look, enhancing accessibility and readability for everyone.

Colour Palette

The app utilizes a color palette consisting of five vibrant colors as accent and a black-and-white scheme for primary colours. This choice of colors aims to ensure high contrast and improved visibility, catering to all users, including those with color blindness or visual impairments, enabling them to efficiently perceive information and navigate through the interface. Primary colours black and white helps maintain a strong contrast, allowing users to easily distinguish different elements and buttons, thus enhancing overall accessibility and user experience.

Illustrations
